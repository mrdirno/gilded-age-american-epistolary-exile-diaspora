## The Story

A woman's banishment becomes a journey of self-discovery through letters, linking the fading opulence of America to the rigid class of Victorian England.

## Entry Points for Contributors

**Start anywhere. Fork freely. No permission needed.**

### Story Entry Points

- Write a letter from Mrs. O'Hara to Elara, sent six months after her departure, describing what became of the brownstone and the neighborhood gossip.
- Compose the single diary entry of Silas Vance written on the night he sent the ticket and bank draft.
- Draft the 'letter of character' provided by the lawyer, Mr. Henderson, to Alistair Finch, revealing what New York society whispers about the Vance family.
- Write a letter from another 'companion' in London, a Scottish woman displaced by the Clearances, offering Elara a different perspective on exile and service.

### Technical Entry Points

- Build an interactive map tracing Elara's journey from New York to London, with clickable locations that reveal fragments of letters, historical context, or sensory descriptions.
- Create a 'Gilded Age Letter Generator' that produces period-appropriate correspondence based on variables like social class, relationship, and underlying tension, using vocabulary from the novel.
